Part 1

1. Verdict

The Meta account is effectively dormant for lead generation, and has been for months. Over the last 90 days it spent A$686.77 and produced zero tracked booked-appointment conversions. The only campaign currently live is a low-budget boosted Instagram post optimised for Instagram-profile visits — an objective that cannot produce an appointment. Every structured lead campaign that could book a patient is paused.

When those lead campaigns last ran (March–April 2026), they were working against themselves: optimising to a soft "call-button-click" pixel proxy rather than a real booking, targeting a manual interest stack that included gamer interests (PC Gamer, PlayStation, Fortnite, Console gamers) on an optometry booking ad, with Advantage+ audience switched off. None of the four campaign roles is running. Retargeting audiences are built but starved (≈20 people each) and idle. There is no Conversions-API / booking-event measurement.

In short: Meta is contributing nothing to the 40→60 weekly-appointment goal. The fix is not to tune what's there — it's a clean, budget-aware rebuild that optimises to the GHL booking event via CAPI, with the practice's recall database (the single biggest revenue lever in the blueprint) as the retargeting seed.

Overall account health: 2.0 / 10.

P0-2 · There is no booking-event measurement — so Meta has been optimising to a click, not a patient. The lead campaigns optimised to a soft custom pixel conversion (CallButtonClick, conversion_id 30607057548908501). A call-button click is not a booked appointment. P0-3 · Kill the irrelevant targeting before any relaunch. The A$366.88 lead campaign ran a manual interest stack containing PC Gamer, PlayStation, Fortnite, Console gamers on adsets named "Booking appointment" and "Prescription Glasses", with Advantage+ audience switched off (advantage_audience: 0). Manual narrowing on Advantage+ costs 20–40% in 2026, and these particular interests are simply wrong for an optometry booking. It returned 0 confirmed bookings. P1 — Architecture + measurement (next 2–4 weeks)

P1-1 · Rebuild to a budget-aware Hybrid structure. Lynbrook is a Hybrid archetype (Google Search leads acquisition; Meta should carry prospecting + the lifecycle/retargeting layer). At the current ~A$7/day Meta budget you cannot run all four roles and clear the ~50-conversions/week learning threshold — so do not split four ways yet. Sequence it:

  • Phase 1 (now, ~A$15–20/day): one Retargeting campaign off the CRM recall list + website visitors, LP-traffic to a tailored booking page, optimised to the GHL booking event via CAPI. Highest ROI for a small budget on a search-led account.
  • Phase 2 (when budget supports it): add Prospecting (Advantage+ broad) for Avatar 1 (paediatric) timed to the Aug–Sep back-to-school search peak, then Testing + Scaling.

P1-2 · Activate retargeting off the recall database — the blueprint's #1 revenue lever. The "2025 Health Fund Campaign" CRM list (≈1,000, ready) plus website-visitor audiences are exactly the seed the blueprint calls the largest single revenue lever (current 5% recall vs 41% industry = 8× headroom). They are built and sitting idle. Re-feed the starved website audiences (≈20 people each — they're empty because almost no paid traffic has hit the site) by driving paid LP traffic, and upload a fresh CRM export for the recall segment.

P1-3 · Clean the account. Archive the 38-campaign graveyard (2020–2026) so the account shows only the live roles. It's cosmetic but it removes the risk of an old A$60/day CBO campaign being un-paused by accident.

P1-4 · Set the benchmark + monitor measurement quality. Target ≤ A$30–40 cost per booked-appointment lead (aligned to the Google cost/conversion baseline A$35.42 → A$30 target, and the ophthalmology healthcare CPL benchmark of ~A$30). Once CAPI is live, monitor Event Match Quality ≥ 6.0 and deduplication ≥ 90% weekly in Events Manager. (Benchmark figures are industry references, not account metrics — see sources.)

P2 — Creative + scale

P2-1 · Creative is brand/style only — none of the six lead-gen angles. The boosted posts ("New season, new style", "high quality, high class") sell aesthetics, not a reason to book. A lead launch needs 15–25 videos + 10–15 statics, refreshed every 4–6 weeks, covering Trust / Qualification / Friction-reduction / Expert / Outcome-first / Risk-reversal. The companion creative pack (this cycle) delivers a first set of three concepts across those angles for Avatars 1 & 2 — a starting point, not the full launch volume.

P2-2 · AHPRA compliance gates the $-offer creative. Four P1 blockers remain open and must close before any priced-offer ad runs (the $199-complete-glasses and 50%-off-second-pair offers): AHPRA registration number, professional-indemnity insurer + policy number, the empty /privacy/ page, and the missing /special-offers/terms page. Every priced or discounted ad needs total-price disclosure and a "conditions apply — see [link]" line pointing to that terms page (brandguide §14, rules 1 & 2). Trust/credibility creative (no price, no discount) can run before the blockers close.

Part 6

6. Channel-feed — how Google and Meta should work together

The Google Ads audit (09/06) found Google is the live lead channel (a running Brand campaign plus a recommended high-intent non-brand Search build). Meta is currently feeding nothing back. For a Hybrid account they should interlock:

  • Shared measurement: both channels should optimise to the same GHL booking event. The Google audit already queued "make GHL Booking the primary, counted conversion (value A$90)"; Meta needs the identical event via CAPI. One source of truth for a booked patient.
  • Search → Meta retargeting: people who click a Google Search ad but don't book are the warmest possible Meta retargeting audience — but that loop is broken today because no Meta retargeting campaign is live and the website audiences are starved.
  • Division of labour: Google owns high-intent capture ("optometrist near me", "bulk-billed eye test"); Meta owns the lifecycle layer — reactivating the recall database and prospecting Avatar 1 ahead of the back-to-school window. That lifecycle layer is exactly what's missing.

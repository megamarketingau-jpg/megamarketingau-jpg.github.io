What the numbers say

Working:

  • Reach efficiency is improving. CTR rose from 2.13% (Feb) to 3.11% (May) while CPC fell from A$1.80 to A$1.29 — more engagement for less.
  • Consistent lead volume — roughly 35–41 form leads per full month.
  • May is the strongest month so far: lowest CPC and highest CTR.

Watch:

  • Cost per result is volatile. It climbed Feb→Apr (A$10.51 → A$12.97 → A$14.56) before recovering to ~A$11 in May. April was the least efficient month — worth investigating (creative fatigue / audience).
  • Frequency 2.88 over the period — moderate; the audience is seeing ads ~3×. Refresh/broaden as spend scales.
  • Lead quality not yet measured. These are form submissions — we don’t yet know how many became quotes or installs. Closing this loop with UNIQ’s back-end is the single biggest reporting upgrade.
  • Ads currently target residential only; the business also serves commercial — an untapped segment.
Important — about the “A$1,299” value: the campaign tags each lead with a A$1,299 value (the “CCTV Mega Deal” offer price). This is a configured offer value, not realised revenue, and is deliberately excluded from this report. It must not be read as ROAS or sales until actual closed jobs are confirmed from UNIQ’s records.

Recommended next steps

  • Establish lead → quote → install tracking with UNIQ so future reports show true cost per job and return.
  • Diagnose the April cost-per-result spike (creative fatigue, audience saturation, or seasonality).
  • Test a commercial audience/ad set alongside residential.
  • Refresh creative and broaden targeting to manage frequency as spend scales.
  • Agree a monthly budget and reporting cadence so optimisation targets are explicit.

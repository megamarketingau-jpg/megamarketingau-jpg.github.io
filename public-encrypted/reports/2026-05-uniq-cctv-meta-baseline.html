What the numbers say

Working:

  • Efficiency is improving month on month. CTR rose from 2.13% (Feb) to 3.11% (May) while CPC fell from A$1.80 to A$1.29 — the audience is engaging more for less.
  • Lead cost is stable and low for the security-installer category — ~A$10–14 per enquiry across the whole period.
  • Consistent lead flow: a steady ~35–43 leads per full month.

Watch:

  • Frequency 2.88 over 90 days — moderate; audience is seeing ads ~3× on average. Worth widening or refreshing as we scale.
  • Lead quality not yet measured. These are form submissions — we don't yet know how many became quotes or installs. Closing the loop with UNIQ's back-end is the single biggest reporting upgrade.
  • Ads currently target residential only; the business also serves commercial — an untapped segment.
Important — about the “A$1,299” value: the campaign tags each lead with a A$1,299 value (the “CCTV Mega Deal” offer price). This is a configured offer value, not realised revenue. It must not be read as ROAS or sales until actual closed jobs are confirmed from UNIQ’s records.

Recommended next steps

  • Establish lead → quote → install tracking with UNIQ so future reports show true cost per job and return.
  • Test a commercial audience/ad set alongside residential.
  • Refresh creative and broaden targeting to manage frequency as spend scales.
  • Agree a monthly budget and reporting cadence so optimisation targets are explicit.

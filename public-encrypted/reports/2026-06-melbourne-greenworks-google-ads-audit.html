Part 1

1. Verdict

In its first month the account spent $2,212 and recorded 43 lead actions (calls + form submits) at a blended $51 cost per lead. That headline hides a wide split: one campaign (Landscaping Search) is genuinely strong at $18 per lead, while the rest of the spend went to a Display campaign at $78, an NDIS Search campaign at $182, and a now-paused Garden campaign that burned $743 at $82 before being switched off. The fundamentals a new account needs are mostly in place — conversion tracking is live, negative-keyword lists are built and attached, ad strength is Excellent, and ads point to dedicated landing pages, not the homepage. The problems are allocation and discipline: budget is spread across 12 campaigns (8 now dead), the winner is starved while weak campaigns spend freely, Quality Scores are low (mostly 3/10), NDIS search demand barely exists, and the live ads carry unverified "4.9 / 50+ / 200+" rating claims that the brand guide flags as a publish blocker.

Net: the account is one good campaign wrapped in noise. Concentrate budget on Landscaping, pause Display and rebuild NDIS off search, fix Quality Score, and strip the unverified claims. Done together, the realistic target is a blended $25–35 cost per lead at the same or lower spend.

Part 5

5. Findings

🔴 P0 — Stop the waste (this week)

P0-1 · Pause the Display campaign. $392 this month at $78/lead vs $18 on search, with the softest conversions in the account. Display is the wrong channel for local quote intent. Saving: redirect ~$20/day (~$600/mo) to Search.

P0-2 · Pause the live NDIS Search campaign (20-May) as-is. $364 → 2 leads → $182/lead, against ~20 NDIS searches/mo. It is paying $5 clicks for generic "gardener near me" traffic dressed up as NDIS. Stop it now; rebuild NDIS off-search later (see P2). Saving: ~$9–20/day.

P0-3 · Strip unverified trust claims from all live ads — compliance blocker. Currently running, verbatim: "Rated 4.9 For NDIS Families", "Trusted By 50+ NDIS Families", "Trusted By 200+ Happy Clients", "4.9 Rated Yard Care You Trust", "Rated 4.9 from 50+ reviews", callout "Trusted by 200+ clients", sitelink "4.9 from 50+ Reviews". The brand guide (§11, §12) flags the 4.9 rating, the 50+/200+ counts, and "licensed" as unverified — do not publish as fact until Zafer provides a GBP screenshot for the rating/count and an insurance/licence certificate. Replace with the compliant RSAs below until evidenced.

🟠 P1 — Structure & measurement (next 2 weeks)

P1-1 · Concentrate budget on the winner. Landscaping Search loses 25% of impressions to budget. Lift its daily budget (start ~$50–60, funded by the paused Display/NDIS spend) and keep it the priority. It has proven $18 leads with room to grow.

P1-2 · Fix Quality Score on Landscaping. Main keyword "landscaping" sits at QS 3. Split the single "Ad group 1" into tight themes (e.g. Landscaping, Landscapers near me, Backyard / outdoor design), each with a matching headline set and the right offer page. Tighter keyword→ad→page relevance is what moves QS from 3 toward 7+ — which lowers CPC and recovers a chunk of the 70% rank-lost impression share.

P1-3 · Stop the campaign churn. Eight paused/removed campaigns clutter the account and risk accidental re-enabling. Keep one campaign per theme and iterate inside it (new keywords/ads in new ad groups) rather than launching a new dated campaign each week — every relaunch resets the bidding learning period.

P1-4 · Right-size bidding for low volume. Maximise Conversions needs ~15–30 conversions/month to learn. The $9/day Garden campaign will never get there. Either fold Garden maintenance into a properly funded campaign or start it on Manual/Maximise-Clicks with a CPC cap until it has volume.

P1-5 · Add the generic-but-relevant terms as negatives where they leak. "gardener near me" ($31, 0 conv) and "garden maintenance near me" ($65, 0 conv) are firing inside NDIS/landscaping campaigns where they don't belong. Tighten match types and route them to the correct theme.

🟡 P2 — Hygiene & scale (this month)

  • P2-1 · Clean the account up: remove the 8 dead campaigns once learnings are noted; rename survivors to a clean convention (Search • Landscaping, Search • Garden Maintenance, etc.) instead of DD-Mon || ….
  • P2-2 · Rebuild NDIS off-search: near-zero search demand means NDIS should run via Meta (audience targeting), the support-coordinator flyer already produced, and referral — not Google Search.
  • P2-3 · Fix the conversion setup: drop "YouTube follow-on views" from counted conversions (no video running — it's noise in the bidding signal), and assign rough lead values per type (e.g. landscaping enquiry > maintenance enquiry) so smart bidding can optimise toward higher-value leads.
  • P2-4 · Fix the garbled structured snippet ("Households all routine.", "Best Values with Garden") — replace with real service values; remove the "Best" superlative.
  • P2-5 · Add a small branded campaign ("Melbourne GreenWorks") to defend the brand term cheaply — currently competitors can sit above your own name. Low budget, high ROI.
  • P2-6 · Trial Performance Max only after Search is clean and converting, using verified assets — not before.

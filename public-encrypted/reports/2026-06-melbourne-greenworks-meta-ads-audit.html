Part 1

1. Verdict

In 35 days the account spent $810 and produced roughly 9–11 lead actions (website form submits + Meta "contact" events), of which only ~3 are confirmed form submissions — a blended cost of $75–90 per lead action, or closer to $270 per actual form submit. The measurement backbone is genuinely good for a young account: the Pixel is installed and firing, three custom conversions (landscaping / NDIS / maintenance form submits) are defined, and six retargeting audiences are built. But the account is being run in a way that guarantees it never learns. The only live campaign right now is an Engagement campaign, not a Leads campaign — the two purpose-built Leads campaigns are paused. Budget is $10/day split across 3 ad sets and 17 ads, so no ad set ever reaches the conversion volume Meta needs to exit the learning phase. And there is a concrete setup bug: every ad set's radius target is pointed at "Box Hill, New South Wales" — the Sydney suburb — not Box Hill, Victoria, so a slice of budget is being delivered to the wrong state.

Net: decent plumbing, wrong wiring. The fixes are structural, not creative: run a single Leads campaign (not Engagement), fix the NSW geo, consolidate budget so an ad set can actually learn, and switch on the retargeting audiences that are already built and sitting idle. Same instinct-level problems as the Google account — spin-up / pause churn and budget spread too thin to compound.

Reading it:

Part 4

4. Findings & action queue

🔴 P0 — Fix this week

P0-1 · Switch from Engagement to Leads. Pause the "29-May Engagement Campaign" and run a single Leads campaign optimised for the form-submit conversion (the Leads campaigns already exist — relaunch one cleanly rather than the Engagement one). Engagement spend buys reactions; Leads spend buys form submits. This is the single biggest lever.

P0-2 · Fix the New South Wales geo bug. Every ad set targets a radius around "Box Hill" keyed to New South Wales (region_id 131 = NSW; the Sydney Box Hill) alongside the correct VIC neighbourhoods. Re-pick Box Hill, Victoria (or drop the city-radius entirely and keep the VIC neighbourhoods + a Melbourne-east/south-east radius). Verify in Ads Manager, then exclude NSW. Any spend delivered to Sydney is pure waste.

P0-3 · Consolidate budget so an ad set can learn. Collapse to 1 Leads campaign → 1–2 ad sets → 3–4 strong ads and put real budget behind it (≥$20–30/day). $10/day across 3 ad sets and 17 ads cannot produce the ~50 events/week an ad set needs to exit learning. Fewer, better-funded ad sets beat many starved ones.

🟠 P1 — Next 2 weeks

P1-1 · Switch on the retargeting audiences that are already built. Six website audiences exist and are ready — Landscaping / NDIS / Garden visitors (30d) and form-submitters (30d) — but no campaign uses them. Add a small retargeting ad set (visitors minus converters) with a different, closer-to-the-sale angle. Warm audiences are the cheapest leads in the account and they're idle.

P1-2 · Build lookalikes + upload the CRM list. No lookalike audiences and no customer-list audience exist. Upload the GHL customer/enquiry list and build a 1–3% lookalike of converters — the strongest cold signal Meta has. (Value-based once you assign lead values.)

P1-3 · Widen the audience to cap frequency. Frequency hitting 3.46 on a 5-neighbourhood audience means fatigue. Either broaden geo (correctly, to east/south-east Melbourne) or lean into retargeting. Refresh the ad creative every ~30–45 days regardless.

P1-4 · Stop the campaign churn. Keep one Leads campaign and iterate inside it (new ad sets/creatives) rather than launching a new dated campaign each week — every relaunch restarts the learning phase and nothing compounds.

🟡 P2 — This month

  • P2-1 · Confirm the Conversions API (server-side) is live, not just the browser Pixel — it protects measurement against iOS/cookie loss and is free signal. Check event match quality in Events Manager.
  • P2-2 · Trial Meta Instant Forms against the website-form path for the maintenance + NDIS angles. Native lead forms often lower CPL for local trades (less friction than a site visit), and they feed the same CRM via the existing integration.
  • P2-3 · Add a cost cap (or test Advantage+ campaign budget / CBO) once there's conversion volume, so the algorithm doesn't chase expensive impressions on a tiny audience.
  • P2-4 · Tidy the ad count: 5–6 ads per ad set on this budget is too many. 3–4 distinct angles per ad set is plenty for Meta to find a winner.
  • P2-5 · NDIS angle: unlike Google search (near-zero demand), NDIS does work on Meta via audience targeting — keep it, but keep wording to "support / weekly home help", never "registered provider" or funding-status language (brand guide §14).

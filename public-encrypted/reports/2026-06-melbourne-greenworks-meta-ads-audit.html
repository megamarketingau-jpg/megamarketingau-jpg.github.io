Part 1

1. Verdict

In 35 days the account spent $810 and produced roughly 9–11 lead actions (website form submits + Meta "contact" events), of which only ~3 are confirmed form submissions — a blended ~$75 per lead action, or closer to ~$270 per actual form submit. The measurement plumbing is genuinely good for a young account: the Pixel is firing, three custom conversions (landscaping / NDIS / maintenance form submit) are defined, six retargeting audiences are built, and CTR (2.5–3.6%) shows the creative lands. But measured against the framework the account is missing its entire architecture. The model is four campaigns running in parallel — Prospecting, Scaling, Testing, Retargeting — each with a budget share. This account has none of them: it has five date-stamped campaigns spun up and paused over 35 days, the only live one is an Engagement campaign (the wrong objective), budget is $10/day split across 3 ad sets and 17 ads so nothing exits Meta's learning phase, and the six retargeting audiences that are built are used in zero campaigns. There's also a concrete bug: every ad set's city target is "Box Hill, New South Wales" — the Sydney suburb — not Box Hill, Victoria.

Net: good plumbing, no architecture. The fix is to stop churning campaigns and stand up the four-role structure: a broad Advantage+ Prospecting campaign optimised to the lead event (not Engagement), a Retargeting campaign that finally switches on the audiences already built (and the Google search leads via CAPI), and a small Testing lane to feed winners into Scaling. Fix the NSW geo, put enough budget behind one ad set to learn, and keep LP-traffic (not Instant Forms). Same root problem as the Google account: spin-up/pause churn and budget spread too thin to compound.

The framework runs four campaign roles in parallel, each with a budget share. Here's the account against it:

Retargeting (30d visitors + CRM + 90d engagers)10–20%❌ Audiences built but deployed in zero campaigns

Recommended split (whatever the monthly Meta budget, e.g. at ~$30–40/day): ~65% Prospecting, ~15% Retargeting, ~20% held for Testing → Scaling as winners emerge. Keep LP-traffic conversions throughout — do not switch to Instant Forms (the framework's hard rule: Instant Forms buy cheaper, lower-quality leads that convert to sale worse).

Part 6

6. Channel feed — how Google and Meta should work together

Melbourne GreenWorks runs both channels, so they should be one funnel, not two silos (archetype: Hybrid — real search demand for landscaping + awareness-led demand for maintenance/NDIS):

  • Search owns capture. The Google audit showed Landscaping Search converts at ~$18/lead — that's high-intent demand capture. Keep Google focused there.
  • Meta owns prospecting + lifecycle. Meta creates demand for the awareness-led offers (weekly maintenance, NDIS) where search volume barely exists, and then retargets every Google visitor + CRM lead via CAPI — the warm pool Search generates is Meta's cheapest audience.
  • The CRM (GHL) is the join. Sync offline conversions into both platforms so each optimises to qualified leads, not raw form fills. One lead source of truth, two acquisition engines feeding it.
  • Budget logic: fund Search to capture existing demand, fund Meta Prospecting to create new demand, and let Retargeting (cheap, warm) run continuously across both. Don't let the two channels bid against the same branded intent — Google holds branded capture.

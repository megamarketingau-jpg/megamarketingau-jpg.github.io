Summary

0. Verdict in one paragraph

The account is doing ~$700/month of real spend through just two truly-live engines — a Performance Max campaign (69% of spend) and a Brand Search campaign (30%) — sitting on top of a graveyard of 49 paused/removed/ended campaigns built up over ~4 years. Blended cost-per-conversion is ~$37, which for a clinic whose first-visit value is ≥$370 (and recurring) is economically fine. The problem is not the headline CPA — it's that the most expensive cost-per-conversion in the entire account is your own brand name (~$48), because the Brand campaign is on the one bidding strategy the playbook explicitly forbids for brand. You are overpaying ~3.2× for clicks on "lynbrook optical", letting PMax serve on your own brand unchecked (its brand exclusion points at Specsavers, not you), optimising bids toward lead actions that aren't actually booked appointments, and capturing zero controlled Search presence on the high-intent non-brand terms ("optometrist near me", "eye test near me") that are the real 40→60-appointments lever. Fixing the bidding + structure is low-risk and mostly free; building the missing high-intent Search layer is the growth move.

Part 2

2. Findings, ranked by money + risk

🔴 P0 — Stop the bleed (this week, low-risk, mostly free)

F1. Brand Search is on Maximize Conversions — you're paying 3.2× for your own name.

The live Brand campaign bids Maximize Conversions. Core principle #1 of the playbook: "Branded gets manual CPC… smart bidding on branded inflates CPCs 3–5×." The data proves it exactly:

  • lynbrook optical (EXACT, Quality Score 8) on the smart-bid campaign: 124 clicks, $663.48, = $5.35 CPC.
  • The same keyword on the Manual CPC twin: 12 clicks, $20.22, = $1.68 CPC.
  • third-party keyword data top-of-page bid for "optometrist lynbrook" is $1.58 low / $7.12 high — a brand term should sit near the low end (~$1–2).
  • Estimated waste: ~$455 / 90 days (~$1,820/yr) on brand alone, or equivalently 3× the brand clicks for the same money.

Fix: Move brand to Manual CPC (or Max Conversions with a hard CPC cap ≈ $2.00).

F2. The live Brand campaign is on the wrong bid strategy — switch it to Manual CPC (the ended twin proves the saving).

There is only one live Brand campaign now ("WD | SN - Brand Campaign", Max Conv). Its Manual CPC twin has ended — but earlier in the 90-day window it served the identical lynbrook optical keyword at $1.68 CPC vs $5.35 on the live one. That's your A/B test, already run, on your own account.

Fix: Switch the live Brand campaign from Maximize Conversions → Manual CPC (or keep Max Conv with a hard CPC cap ≈ $2.00). Don't resurrect the ended twin — change the bidding on the campaign that's actually serving. Same brand coverage, ~⅓ the cost-per-click.

**F3. You're losing 35% of your own-brand impressions to budget.**

The Brand campaign's search_budget_lost_impression_share is 34.8% — on your own brand name, where you should hold ~95%+. You're simultaneously overpaying per click and capping out. Once F1 lowers CPC, the existing budget covers far more brand searches.

**F4. PMax has a brand exclusion attached — but it's the competitor list, not your own brand.**

PMax's negative BRAND_LIST points to shared set "Brand - Specsaver", not your "Brand List" (Lynbrook Optical). So PMax can still serve on "lynbrook optical" and cannibalise the Brand campaign (and report cheap brand traffic as PMax "performance"). The playbook: "Excluded brands list set to avoid PMax competing with branded."

Fix: Add Lynbrook Optical (own brand) to the PMax brand exclusion. Keep Specsavers excluded too.

F5. Two campaigns read ENABLED but have ENDED — clean up + don't be misled by status.

"Website traffic - Exp." and "WD | SN - Brand Campaign Manual CPC" both show status = ENABLED but have past end-dates, so they're serving_status = ENDED and spend nothing — not a live waste risk (correcting an earlier read of this report). The only housekeeping: archive them so the account reads honestly, and salvage the genuinely useful keyword themes from "Website traffic - Exp." (it holds broad core terms like "eye test", "optometrist near me", "glasses near me", plus a deprecated Expanded Text Ad) into the new High-Intent Search campaign (F8) rather than rebuilding from scratch.

Fix: Archive both; harvest the reusable keyword themes into F8. No urgency — neither is serving.

🟠 P1 — Measure the right thing + fix structure (this month)

F6. Bidding is trained on the wrong goal — the real booking isn't counted.

Conversion-action audit (12 enabled actions, ~7 primary):

  • Counted + primary (training the algorithm): Calls from ads, Website Call, contact form submit, Book Online — plus two legacy "Smart Campaign" call actions from paused Smart campaigns still flagged primary.
  • NOT counted, NOT primary: "GHL Booking Appointment Conversion" (offline upload, BOOK_APPOINTMENT) — i.e. the actual CRM-confirmed booked appointment is switched off from bidding.

So PMax's "44 conversions" is a blend of clicks-to-call, form submits and on-site button clicks — not verified bookings. The playbook's highest-value signal ("Qualified Lead / Converted Lead via offline-conversion sync") is wired but disabled.

Fix: Make the GHL booked-appointment the primary conversion (counted), demote soft actions (form-start, GA4 clicks) to secondary/observe, and retire the legacy Smart-campaign call actions. Then both PMax and Search optimise toward real bookings.

**F7. No conversion value anywhere → can't move to value-based bidding.**

Every conversion reports value $0. Owner decision (09/06/2026): set conversion value = $90 per booked appointment — applied to the primary booking conversion (and PMax). This gives Google a real value signal, lets you compare campaign quality not just count, and unlocks Maximize Conversion Value / Target ROAS once volume + offline-booking sync (F6) are stable. (Use $90 as the standing value for a booked eye-test/appointment; revisit if you later want to weight glasses-intent bookings higher.)

F8. The high-intent non-brand Search layer is missing entirely.

Every "money" Search campaign (Eye Test, Optometrist, Kids Vision, Local Keywords) is paused. PMax is your only engine for "optometrist near me" / "eye test near me". For a Search-Heavy local clinic that wants 40→60 weekly appointments, leaving the highest-intent searches to PMax's black box is the single biggest growth gap. third-party keyword data confirms the demand + cost (AU national volumes; local slice smaller but same intent):

F9. "Brand" campaign mixes branded + non-branded ad groups.

The Brand campaign contains ad groups named Optometrist, Eye Doctor, Clinic alongside the Brand ad group — the exact "never mix branded + non-branded in one campaign" violation. It's why generic/competitor search terms ("multifocal glasses", "specsaver", "cranbourne eye clinic", "vision radiology near me") leak into a "brand" campaign. Spend impact is currently small (~$8) because those ad groups barely serve, but the structure is wrong and pollutes the brand-IS picture.

Fix: Strip the non-brand ad groups out; brand campaign = brand keywords only. Move the generic themes into F8's High-Intent campaign.

🟡 P2 — Hygiene + scale (next 30–60 days)

F10. Account-level master negative list is empty (0 members).

Negative coverage is otherwise decent — 6 shared lists on Brand (COMPETITORS 194, Generic Negatives 115, NEW Nega KW 319, Information Keywords 38, Doctors Name 6, Competitors-Search-terms 65) and 3 on PMax. But the dedicated account-level negative list has 0 members, so there's no single permanent firewall. Consolidate a master list (career terms, "free", "DIY", jobs, out-of-area suburbs, online-only retailers) and apply account-wide.

F11. Live ad copy carries AHPRA/quality risk — ties to open compliance blockers.

Callout assets include "Best Price Guarantee", "100%Customer Satisfaction", "Lifetime Service Guarante" (absolute/comparative claims) and "Prescription Glasses $199" with no visible T&C link. These intersect the open P1 compliance blockers (brandguide §14: no comparative-price claims without evidence; total-price disclosure; T&Cs required on discounts) and the missing /special-offers/terms page. Plus typos in live-ish assets: "Warrantry", "Guarante", "100%Customer" (no space).

Fix: Scrub absolute/comparative claims, add "Conditions apply — see [terms]" to every offer asset, fix typos. Gate any offer creative on the /special-offers/terms page being built (open P1 blocker).

F12. Account hygiene — 47 dead campaigns.

The 47 paused/removed campaigns (DEV-, CP_, WD | …, dated 2022–2025) make navigation and optimisation slow and inflate the conversion-action list (58 actions, ~46 removed). Archive aggressively; keep only what's live + a clean "Paused — reusable" set.

F13. PMax asset groups — 2 live, 2 paused; plus a policy-limited asset.

Live: "Pediatric Eye Care" + "Optometrist | Eye Tests" (both strong, 6–8 images / 3 logos / 40–50 videos each — well-stocked). Paused: "Eye Wear + Prescription Glasses" (POOR — underpopulated) + "$50 Off / donate-glasses" (a genuinely good community angle, sitting idle). PMax's primary_status is LIMITED — budget-constrained and HAS_ASSET_GROUPS_LIMITED_BY_POLICY (one or more assets disapproved — likely the comparative-claim copy in §6). With 65% of impressions lost to rank (IS only 18%), there's real headroom: resolve the policy strike, rebuild the eyewear asset group to EXCELLENT, and reactivate the $50/community group.

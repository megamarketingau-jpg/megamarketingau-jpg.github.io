2. Why — one root cause, three channels

The system worked; it worked on the wrong people. Nurture fired, Ruby followed up comprehensively, the landing page converted at a normal 2.7%. But:

  • Meta: ~30 of 33 buyers used personal email addresses. Targeting included "Support Worker" job titles; a $1 offer attracts support workers and aspiring providers, not provider decision-makers. One buyer told Ruby directly: "independent support worker, no use for consulting."
  • Cold email: the list was allied-health clinicians — 2 of 4 replies said "we don't work in NDIS". 73% of the list never even sent.
  • SEO (Search Console): our top organic queries are "how to become a plan manager", "how much do NDIS providers get paid", "is ndis business profitable" — the website attracts the same wrong audience organically.

Meanwhile the SIL Policy Pack's first 5 days pulled provider-domain leads at $15.54 CPL against an $800 offer. A regulation-triggered, provider-specific offer does the qualifying that targeting can't. And real provider demand is visible and unserved in search: "ndis shell company regulations" (1,400 impressions, page 2), "ndis practice standards and quality indicators 2026".

Defects found (fix before relaunch): kit delivery failed for 4 of 33 buyers · purchase tag fires on failed payments · UTM chain verified working Meta→GA4 but breaks at the payment-link→CRM handoff (25 of 33 buyers unattributable) · reactivation email/SMS links untagged (58% of landing traffic shows "direct") · purchase events fire inconsistently (13 events vs 32 real orders) · internal test traffic pollutes GA4 · 3 written unsubscribe requests with 0 recorded suppressions (Spam Act — verify).
